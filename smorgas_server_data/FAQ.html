Questions and Answers

 

Q: Why do we need another online alignment web site? Isn’t NCBI’s blast service sufficient?

A: It is all about time and responsiveness. Usability studies have shown that waiting for a few seconds for results is perfectly acceptable, as is waiting half an hour or more for long batch processing jobs. In between, though, resides an “unfavorable valley”, meaning that a few minutes of response time are too long to stare at the screen, yet it is not enough time to get a coffee and a sandwich. Unfortunately, NCBI’s blastp and blastx hit this unfavorable valley spot on. Our original solution was to make protein searches really slow, hence the name “smörgås”, which can loosely be translated from Swedish as “sandwich”. However, our engineers found that too difficult a task, so we made it really fast. In short: we wanted to present blast-quality results at the response time of the much faster blat.

 

Q: Why don’t you use blat then?

A: Because on a large protein database such as nr, smörgås is even faster than blat.

 

Q: I don’t see an e-value. How do you know how good an alignment is?

A: We provide an equivalent, but call it, more traditionally, p-value. Please note that the statistical model used to compute these values do not reflect the molecular reality, and neither the sampling bias of the protein database (the same is true for blast’s e-value), so please use with caution when you are at the lower end of the confidence.

 

Q: Why are the alignments in graphical form upside down?

A: They are not, that is the way we like to look at them, this is how we designed it. On a more technical note, it is easier to count up in an image, rather than counting down. Once we figure out how to do the latter correctly, we will reverse the order.

 

Q: How do I cite this aligner?

A: We are working on a proper publication, please be patient. If you are one of the reviewers, please send back your report within the two weeks that the journal has given you.
